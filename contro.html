The Columbus Controversy

The debate that settles Columbus's legacy and memory.

History Timeline

Blockage of Trade
On May 29, 1453, Constantinople falls under the rule of the Ottoman Empire and the traditional trade route from Europe to Asia is severed. Major European powers now seek a new route to Asia.
1488 — A New Route
Portugal funded the explorer Bartolomeu Dias to help them find a new route to Asia. In 1488 he finally rounded the Cape of Good Hope, near the tip of Africa, and discovered a new route to Asia for Portugal.
1492 — Desperation
King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella of Spain, desperate to beat their rival Portugal, funded Christopher Columbus to help them find a new way to Asia. His plan was to go west around the world to Asia.
The Landing of Columbus
Columbus arrives in the modern day Bahamas with three ships -- the Niña, the Pinta, and the Santa Maria. He meets with the Taino Indians and starts to trade with them. He brings a couple back to Spain with him. Over the next few years, he establishes many colonies for Spain and earns many titles for himself.
The Consequences
Over the next few years, the Taino Indians are almost wiped out (genocide) and many were tortured for gold and riches. Columbus's titles, from "discovering a new route to Asia," were taken away due to his terrible governing style over some colonies, and the many other atrocities towards Native Americans were committed by him.
Columbus Day
In 1937, Columbus Day was officially instated as a federal holiday to honor Christopher Columbus's accomplishments and to help Italian immigrants feel more welcome in the United States.
Today
The Columbus Controversy is at its peak. People are actively debating on whether Columbus Day is worth celebrating or whether Indigenous People's Day makes more sense to celebrate. Follow along as we break down the controversy for you!

What Is the Columbus Controversy?

The Columbus Controversy is an intense debate about whether we should celebrate Columbus. Some people argue that we should not celebrate Columbus and in fact celebrate Indigenous People's Day instead to honor the lives that were lost because of Columbus. The other side argues that we should honor Columbus for creating the America we have today and because he was a beacon for many Italian immigrants. “One of the biggest misconceptions about Columbus is that he was righteous. The truth is that he was wicked and responsible for the rape and murder of innocent indigenous people,” said Killsback.

Our Views

Our main view is that Columbus Day should be replaced by Indigenous Peoples' Day. Here are our views and reasons:

  • Christopher Columbus had significantly evil morals, especially against societies that he believed were “below” his.

  • What he did to the Native Americans created a norm that carried on for generations, and continues today.

  • Columbus tortured indigenous people and caused genocide.

  • Even though our view is opposite we recognize that Columbus should be celebrated as a beacon for Italian immigrants to the U.S.

  • He should be celebrated for a new chapter in world history and for discovering the Americas.

Solutions & Compromises

Christopher Columbus should be honored for discovering and claiming the American continent, beginning a new chapter in world history. However, we shouldn’t honor who he was as a person, and his morals. Therefore, we should also celebrate Indigenous Peoples Day, to pay homage to the Native Americans who lost a lot of land and population in the process, and to make a statement that what Columbus did to them was extremely wrong, and shouldn’t be done again.
